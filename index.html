Welcome to the documentation of my four-month co-op work term experience. As you explore this website, I hope you'll gain insight into my experience, and the objectives I pursued, and the skills I acquired in the realm of STEM education for children.

Employer

Ontario Tech University's (OTU) Faculty of Engineering and Physical Science, formerly known as the University of Ontario Institute of Technology (UOIT) since its inception in 2002, stands among Ontario's premier engineering schools. By 2021, OTU educated about 10,100 undergraduates and 970 graduates, boasting over 25,500 alumni by 2022. In 2014, the faculty embarked on a mission to introduce Kindergarten to Grade 12 students in the Durham Region to engineering through its 'Engineering Outreach' initiative. Within four years, this initiative expanded, engaging over 24,000 students annually. Their core aim is to ignite curiosity in the youth, equipping them to tackle real-world challenges using STEM disciplines, benefiting not only Durham but its neighboring communities.

Goals

Goal 1: Oral Communication

Improving my STEM presentation skills was both rewarding and tough. I realized my nervousness and speed created difficulty for students to understand. Mock presentations were key. The feedback helped improve my tone and storytelling. This boosted my confidence and clarity. I found analogies to be great for simplifying topics and creating a more engaging presentation. Over my work term, I have become more comfortable presenting especially when explaining STEM concepts. I have much more room to improve, but overall I am happy with how my public speaking has improved over the summer!

Goal 2: Creativity in STEM Education

During my work term, I had the chance to bring my own creative ideas to life for various camps and workshops. This was especially crucial for the virtual sessions planned for the summer. Designing engaging and understandable STEM activities posed a unique challenge. One of the most challenging tasks was crafting a virtual camp for children aged 6 to 8. I had to devise activities they could do independently, pushing me to think creatively. A standout activity was the "future phone" session. Here, campers learned about phone technology and its potential future before designing their own phone model using cardboard. This activity was especially well-received by the kids. I'm happy to report that the camps I was involved in consistently received positive feedback, showcasing the campers' sustained interest and engagement throughout the weeks.

Goal 3: Problem-Solving in STEM Education

Having to solve problems was a huge part of my position at engineering outreach. This took its form in many different ways, through developing activities that were engaging and solving issues as they came up in my workshops and camps. Creating engaging activities required me to learn and practice problem-solving strategies, such as the engineering design process which includes brainstorming, designing and testing solutions. I also had to solve problems they came up with on the spot. If a presentation was not working, we were missing material or I had to find a way to better engage the class or a student, I had to learn to act effectively, quickly and confidently. As my work term progressed, my problem-solving improved, and through feedback I learned how to create more engaging activities so the workshop and camps ran more smoothly.

Experience

During my co-op placement, I had the opportunity to engage directly with students in promoting STEM education. My role shifted midway through the term, transitioning from the academic school year to summer break.

May and June - Mobile Design Lab & Curriculum Development:

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • Collaborated with the Mobile Design Lab team to visit schools in the Durham region.
  • Presented and facilitated STEM workshops for students aged 4-14.
  • Assisted in devising new workshops and activities for the upcoming academic year.
  • Spearheaded the creation of summer camp curriculums and weekly schedules in anticipation of the summer sessions.

July and August - c_wonder Community Team & Summer Camps:

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • Transitioned to the c_wonder community team to oversee summer camps for students aged 4-14.
  • Delivered diverse camp themes, including robotics, coding, and engineering.
  • Conducted both virtual and in-person sessions, necessitating the adaptation of the curriculum to each camp's format.

Highlighted Projects:

  • Future of Technology Camp:
    • Designed and executed this virtual camp from the ground up, developing activities and content related to AI and space exploration.
  • Python Camps (Beginner & Intermediate):
    • Formulated and rolled out curricular content covering beginner to intermediate Python concepts.
    • Developed and tested code for various camp activities, including:
      • A space adventure game, utilizing Python
      • A weather app, utilizing Python and TKinter
      • A classic snake game, utilizing Python and PyGame library
      • A portfolio website, utilizing HTML and CSS
    • Supervised coding sessions, offering guidance and troubleshooting coding issues.

Conclusion

My time with Engineering Outreach at OTU has been a significant period of growth and learning for me. Engaging with students, designing STEM lessons, and collaborating with seasoned professionals provided valuable insights. The experiences during this co-op term have not only helped hone my technical skills but also improved my ability to communicate complex ideas effectively. I'm grateful for the opportunities I had to both teach and learn in this unique environment. This experience has added depth to my understanding of STEM education and its broader impacts.
