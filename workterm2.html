Employer

Sonova holdings founded in Stefa, Swisterland in 1947 specializes in hearing care technology. Sonova holds 24% of the 5.4 billion dollar international hearing aid market. I worked in the Test Configuration Systems (TCS) team as a quality assurance analyst. SQA is responsibly for testing and fixing software bugs, translating, and automation testing for Sonova's Pheonix and Pro&Go software. I worked along side 14 other people including other testers, developers and engineers. I also worked closely with other teams, audiology team which is responsible for manifacturing in the ear hearing devices.

Experience

Day to Day

My daily tasks consisted of attending stand-up and sync meetings. Performing quality assurance for Sonova's Phoenix and Pro&Go software. This meant going through manual testing scripts and testing the software and reporting and bugs to our developers. Testing scripts consisted of preparing hearing aids and accessories with different software images and configurations and testing the functionalities such as image version capture, production test (using a sound chamber) etc.

Software Quality Assurance

  • Executed and maintained manual software testing scripts, ensuring quality for Unitron's assistive hearing technologies.
  • Collaborated with Marketing, Audiology, and R&D teams to conduct thorough GUI and functional tests in an Agile environment.
  • Developed proficiency in various software, both in testing and production realms. Acquired comprehensive knowledge of a wide range of hearing aid families and accessories, bolstering my skills in product development and quality assurance.

Agile and Scrum

  • Worked in an Agile environment, collaborating with developers, testers, and engineers to ensure quality and timely delivery of software.
  • Participated in daily scrum meetings, providing updates on progress and discussing any issues.
  • Worked in two-week sprints, completing tasks and testing software within the allotted time.

Audiology

  • Worked with audiologists to test and develop software for hearing aids.
  • Learned about the hearing aid manufacturing process and how the software is used in the process.
  • Learned about the different types of hearing aids and how they are used.

Data Analysis and Visualization Side Project

  • Created a data analysis and visualization side project using Python and associated libraries.
  • Obtained data about hearing aid production test failure by type from the Sonova database as CSV files. Used Python to analyze the data and create visualizations using Matplotlib and Seaborn.
  • Presented the data and visualizations to the TCS team to help them understand the data and make decisions about future software development.

Conclusion

Overall, I had a great experience working at Sonova. I learned a lot about software testing and the hearing aid industry. I also learned a lot about working in an Agile environment and working with other teams. I am very grateful for the opportunity to work at Sonova and I hope to work there again in the future.
