Employer

University of Guelph, School of Computer Science. I was employed as a UI/UX Designer and Software Developer, working on a research-based project that aims to explore how live-streaming platforms can be tailored to specific global fan communities, particularly the K-pop fandom.

Goals

Critical & Creative Thinking - Creativity

My goal was to enhance my creativity through the development of novel live streaming user interfaces. This involved experimenting with interactive features like switchable native chat, private chat, and tabbed navigation for fans. Positive feedback from the team and users validated the effectiveness of these features, and the entire process was documented for future reference.

Global Understanding - Intercultural Competence

I aimed to deepen my understanding of global K-pop fandoms and how to use technology to foster a sense of community. My research into fan dynamics and Korean culture influenced the platform’s design, with features like live fan translations during streams, which enhanced the platform's global appeal. The insights gained were documented in a detailed report on K-pop live streaming platforms.

Critical & Creative Thinking - Inquiry & Analysis

Another goal was to strengthen my understanding of frontend technologies such as React and Node.js. By implementing key features like real-time chat and an interactive schedule for streams, I improved my technical proficiency and received positive feedback from my peers. This collaborative effort was documented in a technical report for future developers.

Experience

Day to Day

My daily tasks revolved around collaborating with a team to design and develop user interfaces for the live streaming platform. I participated in stand-up meetings, handled feedback from team members, and worked on implementing innovative features to enhance user interaction.

Frontend Development

  • Developed and implemented key features using React and Node.js, including real-time chat functionalities and live event schedules.
  • Worked closely with a PhD student to ensure that the platform’s styling was customizable through Tailwind CSS, making it adaptable for future changes.
  • Created documentation for non-technical team members to better understand the technology stack and features implemented.

Cultural Understanding

  • Conducted research on K-pop fandoms and Korean culture to understand how global fans engage with live streaming platforms.
  • Integrated culturally relevant features such as fan-to-fan translation and live polls, which boosted user engagement.

Collaboration

  • Worked alongside a professor and a PhD student to ensure project goals aligned with academic research and user needs.
  • Participated in team discussions to brainstorm new ideas and refine existing user interface elements.

Conclusion

This work term allowed me to apply my technical and creative skills to a real-world project that enhanced user experience and fostered global fan interactions. I gained a deeper understanding of frontend development and cultural dynamics, and I look forward to continuing this work in future terms.
